Scada Device & IoT Hacking

Have you ever wondered how the water that comes out of your faucet is drinkable? or how the electricity that powers your home is delivered to you? It's easy to take these conveniences for granted, behind the scenes, there's a complex system of technology interfacing with machinery working together to make it all possible. At the heart of this system is SCADA – the Supervisory Control and Data Acquisition system

The Heart of Scada

The components in a SCADA architecture are:
1. Supervisory Computers and Servers: These are the brains of any Scada system. They collect information from different parts of the factory and make decisions based on that data.
2. Human-Machine Interface (HMI): An interface to a particular control center. As an operator, see all the information about what's happening in the factory. You can see numbers, graphs, and buttons to control things.
3. Data Acquisition Units/Remote Terminal Units (RTUs): These are sensors that placed in different areas of the factory and collect data from sensors and devices, like temperature sensors or switches.
4. Programmable Logic Controllers (PLCs): These are small computers that control specific devices, machines or processes in the factory. They follow instructions to turn things on or off based on what the supervisory computers say.
5. Data Historian: This system stores information about all of the controls, what happened in the past. It's like looking at old records to see how things went before.
6. Alarming and Event Handling: Think of this like a fire alarm. If something goes wrong, the system can raise an alarm to get your attention.
7. Security and Authentication: This is like having a locked door to keep out unwanted visitors. It makes sure only authorized people can access the system.
8. Redundancy and Failover: Imagine having a backup plan. If something stops working, another part can take over so that things keep running smoothly.
9. ⛔ Remote Access: Access to check on devices even when you're not there physically. Real-Time know what is happening and make decisions remotely.Not recommended.
